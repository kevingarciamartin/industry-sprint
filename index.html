/Hard Skills

  • HTML & CSS

    If you're starting out as a front-end developer, getting comfortable with HTML and CSS is a must. These are your bread and butter for building and styling websites. HTML gives structure to your page, and CSS makes it look good. Without these basics, creating responsive and attractive sites would be pretty tough!

  • JavaScript

    JavaScript is where the fun begins! It’s what makes websites come alive with things like animations, buttons that actually do something, and real-time updates. Once you’re comfortable with JavaScript, you can level up by learning React. React makes it super easy to build fast, interactive sites by letting you reuse pieces of code (called components), which saves time and keeps everything organized. It’s a game changer for any front-end developer

  • Github

    GitHub is your best friend when it comes to keeping your code organized and working with others. It’s a tool that lets you track changes, collaborate with other developers, and manage your projects smoothly. If you’re planning to work on any team, GitHub is a must-learn.

/Soft Skills

  • Teamwork

    Teamwork and collaboration involve working effectively with others to achieve common goals. Successful teamwork requires good communication, trust, and mutual respect among team members. Collaboration means actively sharing ideas, listening to others, and combining strengths to solve problems or complete tasks. It also includes being adaptable, open to feedback, and willing to compromise for the success of the group. Effective collaboration helps achieve better results through collective effort and diverse perspectives.

  • Networking

    Networking and relationship building involve creating and maintaining professional connections that can offer support, opportunities, and knowledge sharing. Effective networking is about engaging with others, whether in person or online, to build mutually beneficial relationships. It requires good communication, authenticity, and follow-through. Building strong relationships helps in career growth, opens up opportunities for collaboration, and fosters a support system within an industry or field.

  • Time Management

    Time management is about organizing your time efficiently to prioritize important tasks. By setting clear goals and creating a schedule, you can break big tasks into smaller, manageable ones. Focus on one task at a time, avoid multitasking, and take regular breaks to stay productive. Delegating tasks when possible and regularly reviewing your progress helps you stay on track and meet deadlines effectively.

  • Problem Solving

    Problem-solving involves identifying an issue, analyzing its causes, and finding effective solutions. It requires critical thinking to break the problem into smaller parts and evaluate potential solutions. Creativity often helps in exploring alternative approaches. Once a solution is chosen, implementing it systematically and monitoring its effectiveness is key. Good problem-solving also includes learning from past challenges and applying those insights to future situations.
